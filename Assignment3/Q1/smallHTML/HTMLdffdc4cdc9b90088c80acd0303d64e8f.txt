</div></div></div><div class="field field-name-body field-type-text-with-summary field-label-hidden"><div class="field-items"><div class="field-item even" property="content:encoded"><p>Twitter has released a <a href="https://blog.twitter.com/en-gb/2017/lfw-london-fashion-week-is-happening-on-twitter" rel="nofollow">new infographic</a> which outlines the findings of their latest research into the fashion-focused audience on the platform.</p>
<p>As part of the lead up to London Fashion Week (17<sup>th</sup> to 21<sup>st</sup> February), Twitter <a href="https://blog.twitter.com/en-gb/2017/lfw-london-fashion-week-is-happening-on-twitter" rel="nofollow">commissioned Nielsen</a> to get an overview of the fashion-related interests and behaviors of Twitter users.</p>
<p>Their research showed that:</p>
<ul><li>76% of Twitter users are interested in fashion and fashion trends</li>
<li>43% of users look to influencers - such as models and designers - for fashion inspiration on the platform</li>
<li>Twitter users are 75% more likely than non-users to buy clothes that are on trend</li>
</ul><p>Those are some pretty compelling stats – while most would associate visual-focused platforms like Pinterest and Instagram with <a href="http://www.socialmediatoday.com/social-business/pinterest-releases-report-beauty-trends-and-opportunities-brands">fashion trends</a>, the data here would suggest that Twitter should also be a key consideration for fashion brands.</p>
