href="http://kff.org/health-reform/poll-finding/kaiser-health-tracking-poll-future-directions-for-the-aca-and-medicaid/?utm_campaign=KFF-2017-February-Tracking-Poll&utm_source=hs_email&utm_medium=email&utm_content=43316422&_hsenc=p2ANqtz--4X4iIz3X-WzEp0caLtkNEklqiHcnsE9ZCWCGQq058LR2ntjwD2WhDXvKjRbz1Vn7dpj56mfoAeFdNIxoa4X2HJNck0Q&_hsmi=43316422" target="_blank">Health Tracking Poll</a> from the Kaiser Family Foundation found that 48% of Americans view the law favorably, compared to 42% who have an unfavorable view. This is the highest level of favorability measured in more than 60 Kaiser Health Tracking Polls conducted since 2010.</div><div class="zn-body__paragraph">The boost comes largely from independents, half of whom view Obamacare favorably compared to 39% who don't. Nearly three-quarters of Democrats continue to view it favorably, while about the same share of Republicans have an unfavorable view.</div><div class="ad ad--epic ad--mobile" data-ad-text="show"><div id="ad_rect_atf_02" class="ad-ad_rect_atf_02 ad-refresh-default"></div></div><ul class="cn cn-list-hierarchical-xs cn--idx-5 cn-zoneAdContainer"></ul><div class="zn-body__paragraph">Also, a<a href="http://www.pewresearch.org/fact-tank/2017/02/23/support-for-2010-health-care-law-reaches-new-high/" target="_blank"> survey from the Pew Research Center</a> found 54% of Americans approve of the Affordable Care Act -- the highest level ever recorded by Pew -- while 43% disapprove. That's up from an even split (48%-47%) in a Pew survey from December, suggesting support for the law may be galvanized by the ongoing public fight over its future. </div><div class="zn-body__paragraph">Still, opinions on Obamacare remain divided.
