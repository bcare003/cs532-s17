<h3 class='post-title entry-title' itemprop='name'>
<a href='http://www.musclepalace.com/2017/02/reg-parks-5x5-workout-program-for.html'>Reg Park's 5x5 Workout Program for Muscle Mass and Strength</a>
</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content' id='post-body-61652982448065356' itemprop='articleBody'>
<br />
<br />
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="https://3.bp.blogspot.com/-N3K3brobBAA/WK7BiAuyRHI/AAAAAAAAA6E/8UGVbj1o33g8IhjBRZ-vGQZb1Psg6P62gCLcB/s1600/reg%2Bpark.jpg" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img alt="reg park 5x5 workout routine" border="0" height="378" src="https://3.bp.blogspot.com/-N3K3brobBAA/WK7BiAuyRHI/AAAAAAAAA6E/8UGVbj1o33g8IhjBRZ-vGQZb1Psg6P62gCLcB/s640/reg%2Bpark.jpg" title="reg park workout" width="640" /></a></div>
<br />
<br />
<br />
<br />
<div style="text-align: justify;">
<span style="font-size: large;">To gain more muscle mass you have to get stronger. If you want to look strong and big you have to try Reg Park's 5x5 Workout Routine. It is great workout routine for size and strength, &nbsp;that many old school bodybuilders used to build their powerful physique and they were much stronger than bodybuilders today.</span><br />
<span style="font-size: large;"><br /></span>
<span style="font-size: large;"><br /></span>
<span style="font-size: medium;"><a href="http://www.muscle-palace.site/2016/11/classic-bodybuilders-vs-modern.html" target="_blank"><span style="font-size: x-large;">Classic Bodybuilders Vs. Modern Bodybuilders</span></a></span></div>
<div style="text-align: justify;">
<span style="font-size: large;"><br /></span></div>
<div style="text-align: justify;">
<span style="font-size: large;"><br /></span></div>
<div style="text-align: justify;">
<span style="font-size: large;">Old school bodybuilders also spent most time performing compound exercises and lifting heavy weights, which recruited more muscle fibers, and it is better for muscle growth. In order to only get stronger with this routine, you shouldn't be in caloric surplus which you need if you want to gain muscle mass and strength with this workout program.</span></div>
<div style="text-align: justify;">
<span style="font-size: large;"><br /></span></div>
<div style="text-align: justify;">
<span style="font-size: large;"><br /></span></div>
<div style="text-align: justify;">
<span style="font-size: large;">Exercises that Reg Park used to do in his training were squats, deadlifts, bench presses, and power cleans. Reg Park believed that training to failure is a mistake, he believed in gradual progression for gains that last longer. The aim of his workout routine is to do 5 sets of 5 reps with the&nbsp;proper weight and if you can do more than 5 reps with that weight then increase the weight on every training day. But, don't increase the weight if you can't lift it for five sets of five reps. First two sets in Reg Park's workout routine were warm ups and last three were primary work sets. For an example, if you lift 200 pounds on bench press it will look like this:</span></div>
<div style="text-align: justify;">
<span style="font-size: large;"><br /></span></div>
<div style="text-align: justify;">
<span style="font-size: large;"><br /></span></div>
<div style="text-align: justify;">
<span style="font-size: large;">1 Set - 5 Reps with 160 pounds</span></div>
<div style="text-align: justify;">
<span style="font-size: large;">2 Set - 5 Reps, 180 pounds</span></div>
<div style="text-align: justify;">
<span style="font-size: large;">3 Set - 5 Reps, 200 pounds</span></div>
<div style="text-align: justify;">
<span style="font-size: large;">4 Set - 5 Reps, 200 pounds</span></div>
<div style="text-align: justify;">
<span style="font-size: large;">5 Set - 5 Reps, 200 pounds</span></div>
<div style="text-align: justify;">
<span style="font-size: large;"><br /></span></div>
<div style="text-align: justify;">
<span style="font-size: large;"><br /></span></div>
<div style="text-align: justify;">
<span style="font-size: large;">First two sets are great for building confidence and last three sets are the real deal. If you can lift more weight in last three sets, it is time to put 5 pounds more on each set. That is the point of Reg Park's progression, more strength with every training day which results in more muscle mass.</span></div>
<div style="text-align: justify;">
<span style="font-size: large;"><br /></span></div>
<div style="text-align: justify;">
