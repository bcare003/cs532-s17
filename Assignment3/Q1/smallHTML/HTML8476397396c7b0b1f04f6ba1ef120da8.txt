the past year.  Here are the worst offenders according to data from the agency's website: </p> <ol> <li> W. Metro Blvd. in Chandler - 4 priority violations</li> <li> E. Baseline Rd. and S. Rural Rd. in Tempe - 4 priority violations</li> </ol> <p><em>Look at the interactive map below to see how all 51 Chipotle restaurants in Maricopa County fared in their recent inspections.  </em></p><p><iframe height="480" src="https://www.google.com/maps/d/embed?mid=zu2bNacaxUcY.kZHWsvPPxvMw" width="640"></iframe> </p><p><span style="font-size: 12px;">A priority violation, according to the Maricopa County Environmental Services Department website, is described as "a provision in Maricopa County Environmental Health Code whose application contributes directly to the elimination, prevention or reduction to an acceptable level, hazards associated with </span>foodborne<span style="font-size: 12px;"> illness or injury and there is no other provision that more directly controls the hazard." </span></p> </div> </div> <footer class="module__footer"> <p class="copyright">Copyright 2015 Scripps Media, Inc.
