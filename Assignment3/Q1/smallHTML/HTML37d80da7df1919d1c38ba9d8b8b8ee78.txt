<div class="c-entry-content">
  <p id="1ybHQQ"><strong>Grace Slick</strong>, the former frontwoman of Jefferson Airplane and Starship, <a href="http://www.forbes.com/sites/bizblog/2017/02/21/why-i-decided-to-license-starships-music-to-chick-fil-a/#456179214a43">is not a fan of fried chicken sandwich mega-chain <strong>Chick-fil-A</strong></a>, because of the <a href="http://www.christianpost.com/news/chick-fil-a-president-says-gods-judgment-coming-because-of-same-sex-marriage-78485/">political leanings</a> of its CEO Dan Cathy, and the company’s previous decision to donate funds to an <a href="http://www.nytimes.com/2012/07/26/us/gay-rights-uproar-over-chick-fil-a-widens.html">anti-LGBQT organization</a>. But the singer agreed to let Chick-fil-A use the Starship song “Nothing’s Gonna Stop Us Now” in a commercial that eventually aired during the Grammy telecast, so that she could take their money and donate it to <a href="http://www.lambdalegal.org/">Lambda Legal</a>, an organization that offers legal support to the LGBQT community and people living with AIDS. </p>
<div id="mhHxYL"><div><div style="left: 0px; width: 100%; height: 0px; position: relative; padding-bottom: 56.2493%;"><iframe src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/bzk1T4CrjxI?wmode=transparent&amp;rel=0&amp;autohide=1&amp;showinfo=0&amp;enablejsapi=1" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen="" style="top: 0px; left: 0px; width: 100%; height: 100%; position: absolute;"></iframe></div></div></div>
<p id="oQ54fT">In an op-ed for <em>Forbes</em>, Slick writes: “[I]nstead of them replacing my song with someone else's and losing this opportunity to strike back at anti-LGBQT forces, I decided to <strong>spend the cash in direct opposition to ‘Check’-fil-A’s causes</strong> — and to make a public example of them, too.” Slick also mentions that she comes from “a time when artists didn’t just sell their soul to the highest bidder, when musicians took a stand, when the message of songs was ‘feed your head,’ not ‘feed your wallet.’” The singer/songwriter urges other musicians to similarly use their “gifts to help stop the forces of bigotry.” </p>
