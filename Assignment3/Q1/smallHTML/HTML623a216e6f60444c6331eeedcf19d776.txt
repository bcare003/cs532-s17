          <h1 class="article-heading left">This Week in Food Tech News: January 13th, 2016&nbsp;</h1>
          <div class="in-article-tag-holder">
            <a class="tag w-button" href="http://www.hazelblog.org/resources#1">Food Technology</a>
            <a class="tag w-button" href="http://www.hazelblog.org/resources#4">HazelBlog Food News</a>
          </div>
          <p class="subtitle">From an unlikely haven for food startups, to the emerging concern with "food safety", we have this week in food-technology news ready to go.</p>
          <div class="author_info_bar">
            <div class="w-row">
              <div class="profile_image w-col w-col-1">
                <img class="main_bar_image" src="https://daks2k3a4ib2z.cloudfront.net/5592e29b654c69dc323aa806/560e953c01b66e250aca7b9d_headshot.jpg">
              </div>
              <div class="profile_info w-col w-col-11">
                <div>Pat Flynn, Co-Founder, <a class="founder_link" target="_blank" href="http://www.hazeltechnologies.com">Hazel Technologies</a><br>January 13, 2016 4:34 pm CST</div>
              </div>
            </div>
          </div>
          <div class="draw_in_holder w-clearfix">
            <div class="top_left_draw_in_text">
              <p class="draw_in_paragraph"><strong data-new-link="true">1. Food Safety may be the next big challenge being addressed by top entrepreneurs.</strong><br>Amina Elahi of the Chicago Tribune reports that following debacles like the <a target="_blank" href="http://knowledge.wharton.upenn.edu/article/how-chipotle-can-keep-its-food-safety-crisis-from-boiling-over/">Chipotle food contamination</a> crisis, consumers are pushing for information on the origins of the food they eat now more than ever before. <br><a target="_blank" href="http://www.chicagotribune.com/bluesky/originals/ct-big-ideas-food-safety-bsi-0116-story.html">&nbsp;"Organic, Local, Sustainable, but can startups make sure food is safe?"<br></a></p>
              <p class="draw_in_paragraph">2. <strong data-new-link="true">Organic Corn one of dominant food trends in 2015, analysts say:</strong><br>Food and beverage analysts at NPD Group reported earlier this week that corn, organic maize to be specific, saw significant uptick in demand in 2015. This trend is indicative of <a target="_blank" href="http://www.alternet.org/food/8-food-trends-watch-2016">a greater movement toward healthy eating</a> by the general public, which is expected to continue in 2016. <br><a target="_blank" href="http://www.marketplace.org/2016/01/01/life/food-trends-watch-2016">Full Report on 2016 Food Trends</a></p>
            </div>
            <div class="top_right_draw_in_image_holer">
              <img alt="Food contamination at fast-food giant Chipotle will only intensify consumer demand for information" class="draw_in_image" src="https://daks2k3a4ib2z.cloudfront.net/5592e29b654c69dc323aa806/5696d0955e3ae9ab1d3facb8_4.jpg">
              <p class="image-text">Food contamination at fast-food giant Chipotle will only intensify consumer demand for more information on the safety, health, and origin of their food.</p>
              <img alt="Organic corn based products are gaining popularity among U.S. consumers in 2016" class="draw_in_image" src="https://daks2k3a4ib2z.cloudfront.net/5592e29b654c69dc323aa806/5696d0a5039d77221a889d13_5.jpg">
              <p class="image-text">Organic corn based products are gaining popularity among U.S. consumers in 2016</p>
            </div>
          </div>
          <p class="spaced_paragraphs"><strong data-new-link="true">3. Madison, Wisconsin an emerging hub for early stage food startups?</strong><br>It could be so. Startups like&nbsp;<a target="_blank" href="http://www.envoynow.co/">EnvoyNow</a> and <a target="_blank" href="http://mrdelivery.com">Mr. Delivery</a>&nbsp;have all either launched in Madison or selected the city as an early expansion location. <br><br>Among these startups, food delivery is one of the most popular niches. Why? Over <a target="_blank" href="http://host.madison.com/ct/business/technology/madison-a-go-to-for-early-stage-food-delivery-startups/article_cdd688b9-c434-549f-b3e3-0e38b538ae2a.html">80% of food deliveries in the U.S. are still made offline</a>.<br><br><a target="_blank" href="http://host.madison.com/ct/business/technology/madison-a-go-to-for-early-stage-food-delivery-startups/article_cdd688b9-c434-549f-b3e3-0e38b538ae2a.html">Early Stage Food Startups are choosing Madison, Wisconsin</a></p>
          <div class="vid w-embed w-video" style="padding-top: 56.17021276595745%;"><iframe class="embedly-embed" src="//cdn.embedly.com/widgets/media.html?src=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.youtube.com%2Fembed%2FWY97qjaFX4o%3Ffeature%3Doembed&amp;url=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.youtube.com%2Fwatch%3Fv%3DWY97qjaFX4o&amp;image=https%3A%2F%2Fi.ytimg.com%2Fvi%2FWY97qjaFX4o%2Fhqdefault.jpg&amp;key=c4e54deccf4d4ec997a64902e9a30300&amp;type=text%2Fhtml&amp;schema=youtube" scrolling="no" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen=""></iframe></div>
          <p class="image-text">Produce-focused startup appeared on NBC's Shark Tank in a recent episode, and secured $100,000 in funding in addition to a heavy dose of press coverage</p>
          <p class="spaced_paragraphs"><strong data-new-link="true">4. Produce Waste Prevention Startup Secures investment on popular TV Show Shark Tank&nbsp;</strong><br>Produce waste preventing startup Hungry Harvest successfully secured funding on NBC's hit TV show Shark Tank in a recent episode. <br><br><a href="http://www.hungryharvest.net/" target="_blank">Hungry Harvest</a>, founded by Evan Lutz, sells produce deemed cosmetically undesirable for grocery stores at a 20-30% discount. <br><br>According to NPR, <a href="http://www.npr.org/sections/thesalt/2016/01/08/462429929/ugly-fruit-is-ripe-for-a-close-up-as-shark-tank-takes-on-food-waste">Hungry Harvest has saved over 300,000 pounds of produce from waste</a> since the company's creation in 2014. <br><br><a target="_blank" href="http://www.npr.org/sections/thesalt/2016/01/08/462429929/ugly-fruit-is-ripe-for-a-close-up-as-shark-tank-takes-on-food-waste">Full Profile on Produce Waste Prevention Startup Hungry Harvest</a></p>
