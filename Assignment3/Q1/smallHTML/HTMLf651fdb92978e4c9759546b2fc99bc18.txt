<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">The ever-trustworthy Ming-Chi Kuo of KGI securities is at it again with fresh iPhone 8 news, this time about a possible camera update.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">According to Kuo, <a href="https://9to5mac.com/2017/02/21/kgi-oled-iphone-8-to-feature-revolutionary-3d-sensing-front-camera-detect-depth-for-games-and-facial-recognition/" target="_blank">via 9to5Mac</a>, Apple will incorporate a 3D sensing front-facing camera in the next iPhone. This would require the addition of an IR module and an improved front-facing camera. &nbsp;</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Not dissimilar to the Lumia 950 XL’s dual-sensor setup, the new camera would be able to capture a 3D image of the user, which will have multiple uses including biometric security and AR gaming.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">The innovation is the result of previous acquisitions PrimeSense, which will power the new tech, and, Lumentum’s IR technology. The <a href="https://www.forbes.com/sites/jaymcgregor/2017/02/20/iphone-8-facial-recognition-needs-to-be-perfect/#71096e0e367c">recent acquisition of Israeli startup, Realface</a>, which specialises in facial recognition technology, also comes into clearer focus with today’s news. </span></p>
<div id="inread" article-ad-inbody="inread" class="inread"></div>
<p><b>Apple gets a new trick</b></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">The most interesting aspect of KGI’s report is the assertion that Android is “years” behind Apple in terms of 3D algorithms. The exact quote reads,</span></p>
<blockquote> 
 <p><span style="font-weight: 400;">“The analyst says Apple is years ahead of Android platform in terms of 3D algorithms which will make the advanced 3D camera sensor a unique iPhone feature for a couple of years.”</span></p> 
</blockquote>
<div class="vestpocket" vest-pocket=""></div>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">If this report is accurate, and Apple is staking its individuality on 3D algorithms, then it’s a smart direction to head in for two obvious reasons.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Firstly, the AR market has had a huge injection in the last year thanks to <em>Pok&eacute;mon GO</em>. Millions now know, understand and are comfortable with the AR concept and would be open to new innovations and smartphone-related features.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;"> If Apple is planning on heavily focusing on AR and 3D tech in the iPhone 8, then it couldn’t have picked a better time than now thanks to the Pok&eacute;mon&nbsp;craze. </span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Secondly, the reasons for buying an iPhone over an Android - or vice versa - are dwindling. All manufacturers need something to stand out. The operating systems are matched in features and design, whilst there’s little between the iPhone 7 and recent top Android handsets in performance and design. </span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">So a solid, genuinely innovative feature like fast and accurate biometric security facial recognition, or AR-inspired gaming, would certainly set the iPhone 8 against the competition.</span></p>
<p>There are a few hurdles to clear, however, when it comes to facial recognition as a security feature. Namely inaccuracy and, as a result, speed. The Lumia 950XL suffered badly because of it, but I suspect Apple will have a better go of it, especially if the plan is to <a href="https://www.forbes.com/sites/jaymcgregor/2017/02/20/iphone-8-facial-recognition-needs-to-be-perfect/#71096e0e367c">replace Touch ID</a>.</p>
<p>Between <a href="https://www.forbes.com/sites/jaymcgregor/2017/02/06/samsung-edges-closer-to-a-foldable-phone-in-new-images/#45fc6f7210ab">Samsung’s foldable phone</a> and Apple’s new AR technology, 2017 is shaping up to be a year of smartphone innovation. Something the industry has lacked in recent in recent times.</p>
