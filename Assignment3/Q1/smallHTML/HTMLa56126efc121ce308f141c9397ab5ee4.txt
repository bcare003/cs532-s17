<div id="attachment_2062" class="wp-caption alignnone"> 
 <img class="size-large wp-image-2062" src="//blogs-images.forbes.com/blakeoestriecher/files/2016/02/maxresdefault-41-1200x675.jpg?width=960" alt="(Photo from WWE)" data-height="675" data-width="1200" /> 
 <p class="wp-caption-text">(Photo from WWE)</p>

<p>Yes, it's true that, despite what critics will say, Reigns vs. Triple H packs a star power punch, featuring one of the most accomplish WWE superstars of all-time and one of its top babyfaces. But Triple H vs. Reigns is a bout built on a long-running feud that WWE fans have hated from the start, a rivalry in which Triple H, the heel, finds himself being cheered while Reigns, the supposed babyface, is being booed so much it makes John Cena's lukewarm reactions pale in comparison.</p>
<p>Likewise, The Undertaker vs. Shane McMahon isn't without its faults either. McMahon's return on&nbsp;<em>Monday Night Raw</em>&nbsp;and the resulting announcement of his Hell in a Cell match against The Undertaker came completely out of left field, and the match proves that the WWE is desperately attempting to give fans one more reason to tune into WrestleMania 32.</p>
<div id="inread" article-ad-inbody="inread" class="inread"></div>
<p>While McMahon's involvement has people talking and is certainly quite the attraction, it's random, it's somewhat illogical and it forces the WWE to portray the legendary Undertaker as a heel while pairing him with Vince McMahon even though there is no real rhyme or reason behind it.</p>
<p>Will Undertaker vs. Shane-O-Mac draw more fans to WrestleMania? Absolutely. <span class="quotecard" article-quote-card="" data-ticker="TWTR" data-exchange="NYSE" data-type="organization" data-naturalid="fred/company/102047" data-quotes-closing="16.03" data-quotes-now="15.905" data-link="/companies/twitter/" data-name="Twitter"></span> is all abuzz about&nbsp;the match, and odds are that your typical WWE fan is, too.</p>

<p>But that doesn't make it the show's main event.&nbsp;Whether the WWE realizes it or not, the real main event of WrestleMania 32 is Brock Lesnar vs. Dean Ambrose.</p>
<p>That's because Lesnar vs. Ambrose has what Undertaker vs. McMahon and Reigns vs. Triple H don't have yet: True passion and genuine emotion.</p>
<p>It's not believable that Triple H is trying to &quot;hold down&quot; Reigns when just about any hardcore WWE fan can tell you that the exact opposite is true, and in reality, Reigns is &quot;Triple H's guy.&quot; Though it's very, very interesting, Undertaker vs. McMahon came completely out of the blue and doesn't have the gripping hook that the developing rivalry between Ambrose and Lesnar
