And another person with knowledge of Mr. Bannon’s current outreach said the White House official is personally calling his contacts in Rome for thoughts on who should be the Trump administration’s ambassador to the Holy See.</p><p class="story-body-text story-content" data-para-count="264" data-total-count="4730">During Mr. Bannon’s April 2014 trip he courted Edward Pentin, a leading conservative Vatican reporter, as a potential correspondent in Rome for Breitbart, the website that is popular with the alt-right, a far-right movement that has attracted white supremacists.</p><div id="story-ad--aggro-10" class="story-ad ad ad-placeholder nocontent robots-nocontent  ad-aggro-10">

Chief among those battles, Mr. Pentin said, was Mr. Bannon’s focus on countering a “cultural Marxism” that had seeped into the church.</p><p class="story-body-text story-content" data-para-count="256" data-total-count="5340">Since that visit and the meeting with Cardinal Burke — an experience that Daniel Fluette, the head of production for Breitbart, described as “incredibly powerful” for Mr. Bannon — Mr. Trump’s ideological strategist has maintained a focus on Rome.</p><div id="story-ad--aggro-12" class="story-ad ad ad-placeholder nocontent robots-nocontent  ad-aggro-4 ad-aggro-6">

<p class="story-body-text story-content" data-para-count="365" data-total-count="6581">Discussing a Breitbart article about the new mayor of London titled “Pope Hails Election of Sadiq Khan, Celebrates Mass Muslim Migration Into Europe,” Mr. Bannon suggested that the pope “seems almost to be putting the responsibility on the working men and women of Italy and Europe et cetera, that they have to go out of their way to accommodate” migration.</p><p class="story-body-text story-content" data-para-count="92" data-total-count="6673">Was the pope a global elitist, Mr. Bannon asked, “two or three steps removed from this?”</p><p class="story-body-text story-content" data-para-count="180" data-total-count="6853">Many critics of Francis express similar views, but they are often scared to express it for fear of retribution from the pope, who, they say, has eyes and ears all over the Vatican.</p><div id="story-ad--aggro-16" class="story-ad ad ad-placeholder nocontent robots-nocontent  ad-aggro-4 ad-aggro-8">

Or they spread a YouTube video critiquing the pope and his exhortation on love in the family, “Amoris Laetitia,” which many traditionalists consider Francis’ opening salvo against the doctrine of the church. Set to the music of “That’s Amore,” an aggrieved crooner sings, “When will we all be freed from this cruel tyranny, that’s Amoris” and “It’s the climate of fear engineered for four years, that’s Amoris.”</p><figure id="media-100000004916078" class="media photo embedded layout-large-horizontal media-100000004916078 ratio-tall" data-media-action="modal" itemprop="associatedMedia" itemscope itemid="https://static01.nyt.com/images/2017/02/07/world/07bannonpope-2/07bannonpope-2-master675.jpg" itemtype="http://schema.org/ImageObject" aria-label="media" role="group">

Cardinal Burke and Mr. Bannon declined to comment for this article.</p><p class="story-body-text story-content" data-para-count="549" data-total-count="8564">Just weeks ago, the pope stripped Cardinal Burke of his remaining institutional influence after a <a href="https://www.nytimes.com/2017/01/28/world/europe/ten-centuries-later-a-pope-and-knights-do-battle.html">scandal exploded at the Knights of Malta</a>, a nearly 1,000-year-old chivalrous order where he had been exiled as a liaison to the Vatican. The pope had removed the order’s grand master after he showed disobedience to the pope. There was a sense in the order that the grand master followed the lead of Cardinal Burke because he projected authority, a power that stemmed in part from his support by the Trump administration, one influential knight said.</p><div id="story-ad--aggro-20" class="story-ad ad ad-placeholder nocontent robots-nocontent  ad-aggro-4 ad-aggro-10">

Under Mr. Bannon, Breitbart News urged its Rome correspondent to write sympathetically about him. And at a meeting before last month’s anti-abortion March for Life rally in Washington, Cardinal Burke received the Law of Life Achievement, or Nail award, a framed replica of the nail used to hold the feet of Christ to the cross. According to John-Henry Westen, the editor of <a href="https://www.lifesitenews.com/news/cardinal-burke-awarded-by-u.s.-pro-life-leaders-for-defending-faith-life-an">Life Site News</a>, who announced the award, the prize is awarded to Christians “who have received a stab in the back.”</p><p class="story-body-text story-content" data-para-count="109" data-total-count="9244">Despite Mr. Bannon’s inroads in Rome, Mr. Burke and other traditionalists are not ascendant in the Vatican.</p><p class="story-body-text story-content" data-para-count="211" data-total-count="9455">The Rev. Antonio Spadaro, a Jesuit priest who edits the Vatican-approved journal La Civilta Cattolica and who is close to the pope, dismissed their criticism as the stuff of a noisy but small “echo chamber.”</p><p class="story-body-text story-content" data-para-count="177" data-total-count="9632">He also played down the effect of Mr. Trump’s ascent on the standing of Francis’ opponents in the Vatican, saying it was only on a “level of image” and “propaganda.”</p><div id="story-ad--aggro-24" class="story-ad ad ad-placeholder nocontent robots-nocontent  ad-aggro-4 ad-aggro-6 ad-aggro-8">

The pope, Father Spadaro said, is doing everything he can to avoid the clash of civilizations that both fundamentalist Muslims and Christians want.</p><p class="story-body-text story-content" data-para-count="50" data-total-count="10166">Indeed, the pope does not seem to be slowing down.</p><p class="story-body-text story-content" data-para-count="250" data-total-count="10416">Days after the election of Mr. Trump, in St. Peter’s Basilica, the Vatican officially elevated new cardinals selected by Pope Francis who reflected the pope’s emphasis on an inclusive church — far from the worldview of Mr. Bannon and Mr. Burke.</p><p class="story-body-text story-content" data-para-count="260" data-total-count="10676">“It’s not that he is just bringing new people in that think maybe like him,” Cardinal Blase Cupich, the influential new cardinal of Chicago, said after the ceremony. “He is transforming the church in making us rethink how we have done things before.”</p><div id="story-ad--aggro-28" class="story-ad ad ad-placeholder nocontent robots-nocontent  ad-aggro-4">

Asked whether the ascent of Mr. Trump would embolden Mr. Bannon’s allies in the Vatican to intensify their opposition and force the pope to take a more orthodox line, he shrugged.</p><p class="story-body-text story-content" data-para-count="269" data-total-count="11424">“The doctrine is secure,” he said, adding that the mission of the church was more to safeguard the poor.
