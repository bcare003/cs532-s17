Seth Rollins reportedly ‘good for WrestleMania’

   [33]New, [34]40 comments
   by [35]Sean Rueter[36]@s1rude Feb 7, 2017, 11:11am EST
     * [37]tweet
     * [38]share
     * [39]pin
     * [40]Rec

   [javascript] WWE.com

   The backstage story of [41]Seth Rollins’ injury has moved fast, but the
   latest update is good news for Seth, his fans and WWE.

   According to Dave Meltzer on the [42]latest Wrestling Observer Radio
   (subscription required but recommended), reports are indicating Rollins
   will be able to rehab his re-injured right knee in time to work his
   planned WrestleMania 33 match against Triple H:

     Seth is considered good for WrestleMania. So Triple H vs. Seth is
     on, and that's what they're working towards. Maybe that's why they
     didn't tip their hand, but they are going with the idea that that
     match is still on and he will be ready for WrestleMania.

   [43]The Observer reported last week that the diagnosis Rollins received
   at the Andrews Clinic in Birmingham, Alabama was of a MCL tear with an
   estimated recovery window of eight weeks - right around Mania.

   Many speculated that [44]WWE’s update on the Architect last night (Feb.
   6) on Raw only utilizing repackaged existing information and being kept
   intentionally vague was done to keep their options open. If Meltzer’s
   sources are right, it might also be a way to cover a surprise return to
   restart his feud with The Game.

   We’ll see how it plays out, but it sounds like WWE dodged a bullet
   here, and will be able to hit the next major beat in a story they’ve
   been sitting on since last August.
