This Brand Is Reimagining American-Made Luxury

   New York-based Deveaux is creating high-end fashion right here in the
   States.
   Most Popular
     *

   Created with Sketch.
   By [66]Miles Raymer
   Feb 8, 2017
     *
     *
     *
     *
     *

   On the day before [67]Deveaux's runway show at this year's New York
   Fashion Week: Men's, the Lower Manhattan streets full of conspicuously
   stylish dudes are a mess thanks to the wet, icy snow that the city's
   being pelted with. But inside the upstart luxury label's Tribeca
   showroom, the atmosphere is comfortable and warm.

   All the pieces are finished, all the models' changes have been sorted
   out and hung neatly on racks, and designers Matt Breen, Patrick Doss,
   and Andrea Tsao have time to hang out over a pot of coffee—a pleasant
   change from their NYFW debut last year, which necessitated an emergency
   relocation when the venue canceled on them just hours before their
   models were supposed to walk.
   Advertisement - Continue Reading Below

     *

   Clockwise from left: Matt Breen, Patrick Doss, Christopher Fenimore,
   and Andrea Tsao.

   The trio's young, but they have a casually refined air about them that
   seems remarkably mature. You can say just about the same thing about
   their clothes, too. Their spring/summer '17 collection is confidently
   understated even at its most daring, using a neutral palette and soft
   silhouettes–like cropped drawstring pants matched with tunic-like
   shirts–to mellow out the clever way the pieces play around with
   proportion and symmetry.
   Most Popular

   "There's a rule," Doss says. "If we wouldn't wear it, it's got to go,
   no matter how connected to the idea you are personally."
     *

   Deveaux was born out of a dare–or actually. more like a series of
   dares. Could a retailer like Breen, who'd previously founded SoHo
   menswear boutique Carson Street Clothiers (alongside Brian Trunzo, who
   was an initial partner in Deveaux) make it as a designer? Could a
   scrappy startup break into the men's luxury market? And could they do
   all that while also manufacturing exclusively in the United States?

   "We've faced headwinds from a host of things," Breen admits. For
   instance, the challenge of finding American manufacturers who could
   execute exactly what they wanted. "It requires a lot of hand-holding, a
   lot of back and forth. There's really a lot of steps to that process.
   We want the quality to be like a lot of European designers who are
   making things in Italy or Japan. We want to try and push our
   manufacturers to that point."
   Advertisement - Continue Reading Below

   "We've talked to other designers who've been around for five-plus
   years," Tsao says, "and when we say that one of our main objectives is
   to be American luxury and to be made here, the reaction I generally get
   is, 'You haven't been doing this long enough to realize that that's a
   huge mistake.'"

   "One of our main objectives is to be American luxury and to be made
   here."

   Three seasons in, it seems like it wasn't such a mistake after all. The
   day after our conversation, the trio will send a team of models down
   the Cadillac House runway in a respectably robust assortment of
   menswear (with a few women's looks introduced into the mix) in front of
   hi-def drone footage of the Icelandic landscape, one of the
   collection's key influences.

   The amount of attention it draws will feel like a major step up for
   Deveaux, as will the collection itself. It's a little more conservative
   than spring/summer–lots of dark tones, Scandinavian-inspired knitwear,
   and a couple of fairly straightforward suits–but there's still a sense
   of play hovering around it, expressed through the architectural lines
   of a robe coat, speckled trousers, and an unabashedly '70s
   wide-lapelled shearling coat. Overall it feels like a big step forward
   into maturity, and one that could easily lure in fans of the past
   American luxury greats like Calvin Klein that the trio are working hard
   to emulate.
     *

   The Deveaux team in Iceland.

   Deveaux may be hitting its stride, but its designers haven't stopped
   challenging themselves. Fall/winter also sees the debut of their first
   footwear collection: a zip boot, a Chelsea boot, and a slip-on in three
   different combinations of color and leather (plus a fourth option
   exclusive to the slip-on), produced with the family-owned Canadian
   brand Viberg Boot. The expansion underlines the team's commitment to
   doing things their way, and the boots' quality proves the worth of
   sticking to your principles.

   "I think if you listen to criticism all the time," Doss says, "you'll
   never surprise yourself. There are things sitting on that rack that are
   from the same tanneries, the same mills that Prada, Valentino, and
   Hermès use. They wouldn't be there on the rack if we'd listened."

   "People telling me, 'No, you're insane, you're crazy' is what gets me
   out of bed," says Breen.
