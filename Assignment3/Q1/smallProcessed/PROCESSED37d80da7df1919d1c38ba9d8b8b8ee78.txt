Grace Slick Sticks It to Chick-fil-A ‘60s-Style

   [44]New, [45]2 comments

The Starship singer redirects money from the chain to an LGBTQ-focused
charity

   by [46]Greg Morabito Feb 22, 2017, 9:31am EST
     * [47]tweet
     * [48]share
     * [49]pin

   [javascript] [Grace Slick photo: Gett/[50]David Livingston. Chick-fil-A
   photo: Eater]

   Grace Slick, the former frontwoman of Jefferson Airplane and Starship,
   [51]is not a fan of fried chicken sandwich mega-chain Chick-fil-A,
   because of the [52]political leanings of its CEO Dan Cathy, and the
   company’s previous decision to donate funds to an [53]anti-LGBQT
   organization. But the singer agreed to let Chick-fil-A use the Starship
   song “Nothing’s Gonna Stop Us Now” in a commercial that eventually
   aired during the Grammy telecast, so that she could take their money
   and donate it to [54]Lambda Legal, an organization that offers legal
   support to the LGBQT community and people living with AIDS.

   IFRAME:
   [55]https://www.youtube.com/embed/bzk1T4CrjxI?wmode=transparent&rel=0&a
   utohide=1&showinfo=0&enablejsapi=1

   In an op-ed for Forbes, Slick writes: “[I]nstead of them replacing my
   song with someone else's and losing this opportunity to strike back at
   anti-LGBQT forces, I decided to spend the cash in direct opposition to
   ‘Check’-fil-A’s causes — and to make a public example of them, too.”
   Slick also mentions that she comes from “a time when artists didn’t
   just sell their soul to the highest bidder, when musicians took a
   stand, when the message of songs was ‘feed your head,’ not ‘feed your
   wallet.’” The singer/songwriter urges other musicians to similarly use
   their “gifts to help stop the forces of bigotry.”
